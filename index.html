Welcome to an exploration of Papua New Guinea (PNG), a country rich in biodiversity, culture, and landscapes. Dive into the top travel destinations, learn about local wildlife such as the bird-of-paradise and tree kangaroo, and understand the environmental challenges the region faces.

Top Places to Visit in Papua New Guinea

Sepik River – home to tribal art and crocodile worship
Kokoda Track – historic WWII trail and trekking challenge
Mount Wilhelm – PNG's highest peak with alpine views
Tufi Fjords – dramatic coastal inlets perfect for diving

Flora & Fauna

PNG's forests are home to over 600 species of birds, including the famous birds-of-paradise. The coral reefs around the islands harbor rare marine life. Inland, you can spot tree kangaroos and a variety of endemic orchids.

PNG flora includes unique orchids and tropical plants
Tree kangaroos – unique marsupials adapted to forest life
Bird-of-paradise – iconic species with vibrant plumage
PNG's rainforests – rich biodiversity and unique ecosystems
Cuscus – unique marsupials found in PNG's forests

Cultural Landmarks

The country has hundreds of cultural groups; visits to traditional villages along the Sepik River reveal intricate carvings and ceremonial dances. The Kokoda Track commemorates WWII history and is a pilgrimage for trekkers.

Madang – vibrant coastal town with rich cultural heritage
PNG Highlands Region – diverse culture and stunning landscapes
Sepik River – rich culture with intricate carvings and art
NGI – traditional dance and cultural celebration
Kairuku traditional dance and celebration
Milne Bay – coastal culture and natural beauty

Environmental Issues

Rapid deforestation for logging, mining, and palm oil plantations threatens PNG's rainforests. Coral bleaching from warming seas and pollution are harming marine ecosystems. Conservation efforts by local NGOs work to protect these fragile habitats.

Deforestation – logging and agriculture threaten PNG's forests
Coral bleaching – warming seas harm PNG's marine ecosystems
Conservation efforts – local NGOs work to protect PNG's habitats
Pollution – impacting PNG's pristine waterways
